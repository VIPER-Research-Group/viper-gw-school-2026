Traveling to Vanderbilt

Vanderbilt University is a private university in Nashville, Tennessee. Nashville is sometimes called Music City — most famous for country music, but home to an endless variety of music and entertainment.

When

The school runs July 20 – 31, 2026 (two weeks, Monday–Friday). Plan to arrive the weekend before July 20 and depart on or after Friday July 31.

Venue

All sessions are held in the Chapel auditorium of the 17th & Horton Building (the “Sony Building”) on the Vanderbilt campus. The 17th & Horton Building is the former Sony/BMG building, and most people on campus still call it the “Sony Building.”

Address: 1400 18th Avenue South, Nashville, TN · open in Google Maps

Airport

Nashville International Airport (BNA) is roughly a 15–20 minute drive from Vanderbilt. An Uber or Lyft to or from campus costs about $20–$30.

Lodging

Nashville is a popular destination, so book early — especially for a two-week stay. Options include:

  • Scarritt Bennett Center: a conference center on the edge of campus with no-frills accommodation; suites with shared bathrooms are often available.
  • Airbnb / short-term rentals: entire apartments or houses close to campus, more affordable if shared. Use the participants list to find people to share with.

Getting around

Nashville has a limited bus network. E-scooters are easy to unlock by app and popular for short distances; for longer trips, Uber and Lyft are the most reliable option. Much of Midtown and Downtown is walkable from campus.
