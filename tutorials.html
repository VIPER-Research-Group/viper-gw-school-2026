Tutorials & demos

Hands-on notebooks for the afternoons, plus browser-based demos you can open right now.

Course materials repository

Slides and Jupyter tutorials, organized by week and day — timing a pulsar, GWB searches (frequentist & Bayesian), holodeck, continuous waves, and more. Clone it and git pull for updates.

Interactive browser demos

Self-contained visual demos: GWs from compact binaries, pulse delays along the geodesic, pulsar timing vs. a passing GW, the Hellings & Downs curve, and SMBH-binary light curves. No install needed.

What you’ll work through

  • Timing a pulsar with tempo2, PINT and JUG.
  • Practical statistics & foundational MCMC — from priors to posteriors.
  • Detecting a GW background — both the optimal-statistic (frequentist) and full Bayesian (enterprise) routes.
  • holodeck — simulating the SMBHB population and its background.
  • PTArcade — new-physics interpretations of a nanohertz background.
  • Continuous-wave detection of individual binaries.

Setup instructions are on the installation page. Come with the viper environment already built.
