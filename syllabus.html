Syllabus

What we cover, roughly in the order we cover it.

Week 1 — foundations

  • The GW landscape: gravitational-wave theory and astrophysics across the spectrum; detectors from the ground-based band down to the nanohertz.
  • Pulsars & pulsar timing: what pulsars are, how we time them (tempo2, PINT), and the timing response to gravitational waves.
  • Stochastic GW backgrounds: probing backgrounds through statistics; n-point correlations; the overlap reduction function and the Hellings & Downs curve.
  • Sources & signals: supermassive black-hole binaries as individual sources and as the origin of a GW background; the astrophysics behind the population.
  • A tour of astro-statistics: frequentist and Bayesian inference, parameter estimation, model selection, and foundational MCMC.
  • The PTA likelihood & first detections: the structure of the PTA likelihood and practical recipes for detecting a GW background, both frequentist and Bayesian.

Week 2 — frontier topics & methods

  • New-physics GW signals: cosmological and beyond-Standard-Model sources of a nanohertz background, and how to model them (e.g. PTArcade).
  • Noise: the noise budget of a pulsar timing array and how it shapes inference.
  • Electromagnetic SMBH binaries: multi-messenger and EM signatures of inspiralling supermassive black holes.
  • Advanced sampling: modern samplers and techniques for high-dimensional, expensive PTA posteriors.
  • Population inference: inferring the SMBHB population from a measured background.
  • Continuous waves: searching for individual, resolvable SMBH binaries.
  • Beyond the background: overlap reduction functions, anisotropy, polarization, and tests of general relativity.

Afternoons are dedicated to hands-on tutorials, a software helpdesk, and open hack time — bring your own questions and data. See the schedule for the day-by-day breakdown and the tutorials page for the notebooks.
